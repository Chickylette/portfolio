Junkenstein's Revenge

Junkenstein's Revenge is a classic 4 player co-op event only available during Halloween in Overwatch. The objective is to prevent the gate being destroyed by waves of enemies and bosses. The gamemode has two different modes, a story mode and an endless mode.

Endless Madness

Endless Madness is a custom gamemode built on Overwatch's Endless mode, set to Legendary difficulty. Players will face a total of 12 normal waves followed by 16 bonus waves. The mode introduces several new mechanics to enrich the experience, including a money and shop system, customizable hero stats, special wave events, and the powerful Super Ultimate abilities.

Money System Overview

A core feature of Endless Madness is the use of money as a gameplay resource. Players earn money throughout the match and can spend it to upgrade their hero's stats, purchase game-changing abilities, and unlock unique Super Ultimate powers.

How to Earn Money

  • Blue Circle (near the gate): Standing in this circle grants periodic income. This blue circle will occur at the front of the gate.
  • Passive Income Zones (Green Circles): Passive income is granted over time and can be increased by standing inside green circles placed around the map. Two appear at the start of the game at the corners next to the gate, and a third green circle is introduced after the normal wave phase ends, appearing in the middle of the map.
  • Blood Stone (Red Circle): A high-risk, high-reward mechanic. Players can stand in the red circle to sacrifice their own health in exchange for a large sum of money. This area appears only after the normal waves are completed. It will appear at the hay next to the third green circle.
  • Boss Kills: Defeating bosses also grants bonus money, encouraging players to focus fire and complete waves efficiently.

How to Spend Money (Shop)

Players can access the in-game shop to enhance their performance and customize their playstyle. Available upgrades include:

  • Health – Increases your overall survivability.
  • Healing per second – Boosts health regeneration over time.
  • Healing received/dealt – Enhances incoming and outgoing healing effects.
  • Damage received/dealt – Adjusts damage intake and output for better combat performance.
  • Projectile speed – Improves hit accuracy and reaction time.
  • Movement speed – Increases mobility for dodging or repositioning.
  • Objective multiplier – Amplifies income generation from standing in circles around the map.

The shop also unlocks powerful abilities as the game progresses, including unique options like Lifesteal, Flying, and Super Ultimate abilities. These additions allow for deep strategic builds and player-specific customizations.

Waves and Bosses

Endless Madness maintains the core wave structure of the original mode but introduces dynamically scaling bosses. All non-boss enemies function as they do in the base game, ensuring familiar mechanics. However, boss enemies are scaled based on the current wave, increasing in difficulty, health, and damage output as the game progresses.

Defeating a boss rewards players with a bonus money payout, adding an extra incentive for precise coordination and tactical plays. Each bonus wave also has the potential to introduce special boss modifiers or combinations, keeping the gameplay unpredictable and exciting.

Super Ultimate

The Super Ultimate is a powerful ultimate that strengthens the original. Some of these drastically enhance a hero’s stats, while others unleash custom, cinematic effects.

Every Super Ultimate is uniquely designed and influenced by the creator’s favorite anime and video game inspirations. From flashy multi-stage attacks to transformation sequences, these abilities bring a layer of personal flair and creative storytelling to each hero, making their peak moments feel truly memorable.
